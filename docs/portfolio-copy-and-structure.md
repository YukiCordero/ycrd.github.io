# Portfolio Copy and Structure

This document records the recommended copy and section logic for the Yuki Cordero portfolio website. The positioning is intentionally practical: AI automation and Tech VA support for small businesses that need faster follow-up, cleaner tracking, clearer documentation, and fewer repetitive admin steps.

## Recommended homepage structure

1. Header navigation with one high-intent CTA.
2. Hero section with outcome-driven headline, service positioning, and CTAs.
3. About preview that explains Yuki's practical automation and operations support.
4. Services section organized by business problems, not only tools.
5. Tools section grouped by use case.
6. Process section showing audit, build, test, and document.
7. Projects/case studies with clear evidence and careful claims.
8. Results/highlights section using demonstrable build outputs.
9. Testimonial section.
10. Rate/package section.
11. FAQ section.
12. Contact section with a clear workflow review prompt.
13. Footer with brand mark, copyright, and back-to-top link.

## About section copy

I’m Yuki Cordero, an AI Automation and Tech Virtual Assistant focused on practical systems for small businesses. I combine workflow thinking, automation tools, CRM support, and lightweight web/data skills to help owners reduce manual work and keep important tasks visible.

My work is best suited for teams that need a reliable operator-builder: someone who can understand the existing process, organize the moving parts, set up practical automations, test the workflow, and document the handoff clearly.

## Intended positioning notes

The site should present Yuki as a practical AI Automation VA / Tech VA for small businesses rather than as a generic assistant or a purely technical developer. The strongest positioning themes are:

- AI automation that reduces repetitive admin steps.
- Tech VA support that combines tools, documentation, and operational reliability.
- Lead follow-up systems that help prevent missed inquiries.
- CRM support for cleaner records, stages, notes, and next actions.
- Workflow documentation so systems can be maintained after handoff.
- Small-business operations support across trackers, dashboards, internal tools, reports, and website updates.

Claims should remain evidence-based. Avoid invented ROI, revenue, saved-hours, or client metrics unless the final owner can verify them. Portfolio simulations should remain clearly labeled as simulations, and family-business or internal projects should be described as operational build experience rather than inflated client work.
